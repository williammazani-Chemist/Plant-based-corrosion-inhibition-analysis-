# Corrosion Inhibition of Mild Steel Using Plant Extract

## 📌 Overview
This project investigates the corrosion inhibition performance of a plant extract on mild steel in an acidic medium using gravimetric analysis. The study evaluates the effect of inhibitor concentration on the corrosion rate and weight loss behavior of mild steel.

---

## 🧪 Methodology
- Gravimetric (weight loss) method was used to determine corrosion rate.
- Mild steel specimens were exposed to acidic medium with varying concentrations of plant extract.
- Corrosion inhibition efficiency was analyzed based on mass loss measurements.

---

## 📊 Results & Interpretation

### Interpretation

The weight loss of mild steel decreased as the inhibitor concentration increased, indicating that the plant extract effectively reduced metal dissolution in the acidic medium. This trend suggests that increasing the inhibitor concentration enhanced the formation of a protective adsorbed layer on the mild steel surface, thereby improving corrosion resistance. The observed behavior demonstrates the concentration-dependent corrosion inhibition performance of the plant extract.

## 📈 Key Findings
- Increased inhibitor concentration reduces corrosion rate.
- Plant extract forms a protective adsorption layer on mild steel surface.
- Corrosion inhibition is concentration-dependent.

---

## 🎯 Conclusion
The plant extract shows effective corrosion inhibition properties and can potentially serve as an eco-friendly corrosion inhibitor for mild steel in acidic environments.
